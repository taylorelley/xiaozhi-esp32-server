# An Open Letter to Developers

"When the river warms in spring the ducks are the first to know, and that's just when the pufferfish are about to rise!"

Dear friends, I am John, an ordinary Java programmer at a regular company. Today, with utmost sincerity, I write this open letter to those of you who love AI technology and innovation.

Six months ago, I saw many excellent projects, such as AI-related projects like `Dify` and `Chat2DB`. I thought, how nice it would be if I could contribute to such projects, but sadly, "I had nowhere to serve my country, and wasted ten years of coding."

It was in early 2025 when I stumbled across a video from Brother Xia's team. I was very curious about how they had built it, and I wanted to replicate their backend service and create a low-cost civilian Jarvis. Unfortunately, what I have made so far is still only an "artificial stupidity": low concurrency, no soul, slow response, and full of bugs.

Brother Xia's team is our role model, and I really want to have a LittleWise backend service as smart as theirs. But I can also understand Brother Xia's decision not to open-source it. "A single flower does not make a spring; a hundred flowers in bloom make a garden." The age when AI blossoms everywhere may well be achieved in our generation. With our own hands, we can build a low-cost civilian Jarvis. Personally, I believe that what they have achieved, we can achieve too. It's only a matter of time. I call this "our journey to the West."

So what difficulties will we encounter on this journey? I think there will be no fewer than eighty-one trials. Along the way, all sorts of demons will inevitably appear, but there will also be deities secretly helping us, and people joining our pilgrimage group.

If you find any of the above amusing, then I feel extremely fortunate. If I can make you laugh for five seconds in the more than 30,000 days of your life, that counts as one contribution I have made for you.

Will this idea of a civilian low-cost Jarvis fail? I don't know, but in our ordinary lives, isn't such failure very common?

One thing about the future is certain: someone will eventually fully replicate the features of Brother Xia's team and realize a civilian low-cost Jarvis. Will this project be us?

I look forward to walking forward with you, creating the future together.

John, 2025.3.11, Guangzhou

# Appendix: Contribution Guide
## Project Goals

1. **A civilian low-cost Jarvis solution**

2. **A solution for intelligent integration with surrounding hardware**

## Join Us

We warmly welcome like-minded friends to join us and contribute to the project together. You can view the features we plan to implement in the near future at [this link](https://github.com/users/xinnan-tech/projects/3). Any features in the list that have not been assigned yet are exactly where your participation is urgently needed. Ways to participate:

### 1. Become a regular contributor

Fork the project, submit a PR, and developers will review and merge it into the main branch.

### 2. Become a developer

Once you have accumulated 3 valid PRs, you can contact the group owner to apply to become a developer. The group owner will invite you to join a dedicated developer group to discuss the future of the project together.

## Developer workflow

1. **Create a new branch**
   Please develop each feature point on a new branch. Branch names should be concise and clear, so that the feature being implemented is obvious at a glance, avoiding feature conflicts.

2. **Submit a PR for review**
   After completing feature development, please submit a PR on GitHub. Other developers will review it, and once approved, it will be merged into the main branch.
